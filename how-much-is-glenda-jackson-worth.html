Glenda Jackson Net Worth: How Much Is Glenda Jackson Worth?

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Sunday, May 26, 2024

Glenda Jackson Net Worth: How Much Is Glenda Jackson Worth?: Born on May 9, 1936, Glenda Jackson, officially known as Glenda May Jackson was an English actress and politician.

She developed a passion for acting at a younger age and growing up studied at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art (RADA)

Jackson made her Broadway debut in “Marat/Sade” and consistently rose throughout the ranks to become one of the most sought-after English actresses.

As an actress, she was one of the few artists to achieve the Triple Crown of Acting, having won two Academy Awards, three Emmy Awards, and a Tony Award.

Jackson won the Academy Award for Best Actress twice; for her roles in the romantic drama film; “Women In Love” and romantic comedy film; “A Touch of Class”

For playing the leading role in “Sunday Bloody Sunday”, she won the BAFTA Award for Best Actress in a Leading Role and also a British Academy Television Award for Best Actress for her role in Elizabeth Is Missing.

Jackson won two Primetime Emmy Awards for her role as Elizabeth I in the BBC series Elizabeth R. She’s known for her roles in “Mary, Queen of Scots”, Hedda, The Incredible Sarah, and Hopscotch, to mention a few.

She received five Laurence Olivier Award nominations for her West End roles in the films; “Stevie”, “Rose”, “Strange Interlude”, and “Antony and Cleopatra”, among others.

From 1992 to 2015, she took a break from acting to become a politician and was elected as the Labour Party MP for Hampstead and Highgate in the 1992 general election.

From 1997 to 1999, Jackson served as a junior transport minister during the government of Tony Blair.

Following constituency boundary changes, she represented Hampstead and Kilburn in 2010. Jackson stood down at the 2015 general election and returned to acting.

Sadly, the Oscar-winning actress and former MP has died. Glenda Jackson passed away on Tuesday, June 15, 2023, at her home in Blackheath at the age of 87.

Following her demise, Tributes were paid from across the worlds of the arts and politics.

Tulip Siddiq tweeted: “Devastated to hear that my predecessor Glenda Jackson has died. A formidable politician, an amazing actress and a very supportive mentor to me. Hampstead and Kilburn will miss you Glenda”

Lucy Powell tweeted: “She was the definition of an icon, successfully spanning the world of acting and politics with great aplomb”

Gyles Brandreth tweeted: “A wonderful actress, a committed politician, a remarkable human being – we became MPs on the same day in 1992 & I treasure our unlikely friendship. She was such a gifted, caring & special person who came into the world to make a difference – and did. RIP the unique Glenda Jackson”
