Jeff Probst Biography – Wiki

Jeff Probst is an American game show host and an executive producer born on November 4, 1961, in Wichita, Kansas, United States.

Probst is best known as the Emmy Award-winning host of the U.S. version of the reality television show Survivor. Probst was also the host of The Jeff Probst Show, a syndicated daytime talk show produced by CBS Television Distribution from September the year 2012 to May the year 2013.

Jeff Probst Family

Probst was born at the Wesley Medical Center in Wichita, Kansas to Jerry Probst his father and Barbara Probst his mother, and grew up primarily in Bellevue, Washington.

Probst has two brothers named Brent Probst and Scott Probst. Probst had married to his former wife Shelly Wright, married 1996 to 2001, after separation he married again to another wife, who is now the current wife Lisa Ann Russell American actress married since 2011, she born on 21 March 1972 (age 47 years), Sterling, Illinois, United States.

After graduating from Newport High School in 1979, Probst attended Seattle Pacific University and worked at Boeing Motion Picture/Television studio as a producer as well as the narrator of marketing videos.

Jeff Probst Salary

Probst is an American game show host and an executive producer who earns a salary of $4 million per year as host of the long-running CBS reality show Survivor.

Jeff Probst on survivor – Jeff Probst Stranded

In addition to Survivor, he once hosted FX’s original half-hour show dedicated to answering viewer letters, Backchat, along with Sound FX, a music series featuring Orlando Jones (In the year 1996). He also hosted the VH1 series Rock & Roll Jeopardy! from the year 1998 to 2001, and was a correspondent for the syndicated program Access Hollywood.

On April 1, 2009, he appeared on the CBS reality television special I Get That a Lot, in which he worked a cash register.

In October 2011, Probst appeared as himself on the sitcom How I Met Your Mother, in the episode “The Stinson Missile Crisis”.

In January 2012, he was announced as director of his second feature film, coming-of-age story Kiss Me, starring John Corbett along with Sarah Bolger, with production scheduled to begin in Los Angeles, California, the following month.

From September 10, 2012, he hosted The Jeff Probst Show, a syndicated daytime talk show produced by CBS Television Distribution from September 2012 to May 2013. CBS did not pick up the show for a 2nd season, citing low ratings. Starting in October 2012, he has hosted the recurring Adult Swim special, The Greatest Event in Tv History, which consists of remakes of the year 1980s TV show title sequences.

In February 2013, he teamed up with Christopher Tebbetts to release the first of Scholastic’s adventure series Stranded. Stranded is intended for middle school students, grades 4 to 6. It follows the story of Vanessa, Buzz, Carter, along with Jane as they are left on a deserted island and forced to fend for themselves.

It started out as a regular vacation but when a storm sets in, the children are shipwrecked in the middle of the South Pacific without any parents. They must find a way to work as one if they are ever to get off the island. Stranded is the first of the three-book series.

In November 2013 as well as January 2014, Probst appeared as himself on the sitcom 2 1/2 Men in two season 11 episodes, “Some Kind of Lesbian Zombie” as well as “Baseball. Boobs. Boobs. Baseball.”.

In December 2016, he appeared as himself on the sitcom Life in Pieces, in the episode “Swim Survivor Zen Talk”.
