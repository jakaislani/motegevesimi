You cant touch him, they give him every fu**ing call, Austin Rivers sends out a CONTROVERSI

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Thursday, May 2, 2024

Stephen Curry, the Golden State Warriors‘ superstar is unquestionably one of the NBA’s hardest covers, but Minnesota Timberwolves guard Austin Rivers thinks the officials assist him. Curry was mentioned by Rivers as the NBA player that is hardest to guard during a recent podcast episode, but for an odd reason. At first sight, it appears that Golden State Warriors superstar Stephen Curry was receiving the highest accolades from Minnesota Timberwolves guard Austin Rivers. However, Rivers soon created controversy by suggesting that he gets help from the officials.

Speaking on the podcast, Rivers said, “Steph. It’s not even close… you can’t touch him, they give him every f-ucking call, they set illegal screens for him the entire game… they don’t call it cause they want to see him shoot.“

This one won’t appeal to Warriors fans. Instead of simply criticizing Steph for his outstanding offensive skills and moving on, Rivers accused the NBA and the Warriors of working together to maximize the 2x MVP’s scoring opportunities.

Even if this may be true in many ways, it is still not incorrect. Although Steph is undoubtedly capable of scoring in any manner he chooses, as the league’s representative and one of the most influential NBA players ever. Curry will gain access to benefits that others will never experience. It’s okay that way. As superstars always get some calls that the refs won’t even see if it was any other player. Also, the Greatest Shooter of All-Time knows how to earn his calls by using various tactics be it bumping into the defender while drilling a three-pointer or attacking the rim with some contact being made.

Stephen Curry revolutionized the NBA with his shooting greats

Before Curry’s breakthrough season with the Warriors, no one could shoot like he could. The league was completely taken aback by what he was doing at the time, and many people are still having trouble understanding it today. As a smaller guard without a physical advantage to dominate the opponents, it is far more difficult to lead your team to a championship. Stephen Curry is among the greatest players ever because he broke the mold in a game of giants

Curry’s high volume of three-point attempts, which suggests his play-style naturally avoids contact and trips to the line, is sometimes used to refute criticism of his low free throw attempt rate.
