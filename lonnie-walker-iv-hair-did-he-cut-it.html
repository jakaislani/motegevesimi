People are curious about the Lonnie Walker IV haircut. For a very long time, Lonnie has always had various, unique hairstyles.

Walker plays basketball for the National Basketball Association Los Angeles Lakers.

He played high school basketball for Pennsylvania’s Reading High School. Walker committed to continuing his academic and athletic careers at the University of Miami by signing a letter of intent. Walker was chosen as a McDonald’s All-American in January 2017.

How Long Is Lonnie Walker IV Hair Now: Did He Cut It?

Lonnie Walker IV, a talented young guard for the Spurs, shocked many when he shaved off his signature pineapple hairstyle, which he had been growing since the fifth grade.

Walker cuts his hair as part of his struggle with childhood sexual abuse.

In his Instagram post, Walker revealed why he decided to let his hair grow out as a teenager to make what he called “a cloaking device” to numb the discomfort of having been sexually abused as a youngster.

For Spurs supporters, getting such an intimate insight into players’ personal lives is comparatively rare, so this hurts the heart.

Although his background in hardship in Reading, Pennsylvania, was a somewhat well-known truth, this brings it to a whole new level, and we can’t help but love Lonnie even more for achieving victory.

He has always felt like a Spur-for-life, and hopefully, cutting his hair is the beginning of an amazing new journey for him.

Walker’s new hairstyle includes a cross-shaped shave in the back where his fade begins and a curved part in the front.
